Classic, Comforting, and Crowd-Pleasing

This vegetarian lasagna is a satisfying, flavor-packed twist on the Italian classic—layered with roasted vegetables, creamy ricotta, tangy marinara, and melty mozzarella. Each bite delivers the perfect balance of richness and freshness, making it a crowd-pleaser even among meat lovers.

Perfect for weeknight dinners, meal prep, or feeding a hungry group, this meat-free lasagna is both nourishing and indulgent. With simple, wholesome ingredients and a customizable veggie filling, it is a comforting dish you'll turn to again and again.

Ingredients

For the Vegetable Filling

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 medium yellow squash, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small eggplant, diced
  • 1 cup baby spinach (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs (or a mix of oregano, basil, thyme)

For the Cheese Mixture

  • 1 ½ cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg (optional, for binding)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil or parsley, chopped

Other Layers

  • 9 lasagna noodles (regular or no-boil)
  • 3 cups marinara sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese

Optional Additions

  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (sautéed with the veggies)
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes (for a little heat)
  • Vegan alternatives for ricotta, Parmesan, and mozzarella, if needed

Instructions

1. Prepare the Vegetables

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the diced zucchini, squash, bell pepper, and eggplant with olive oil, salt, pepper, and dried Italian herbs.
  3. Spread the vegetables on a baking sheet and roast for 20 or 25 minutes, until tender and slightly caramelized. Stir halfway through.
  4. (Optional) In the last 5 minutes, toss in the spinach to wilt slightly, or sauté it separately with garlic until wilted.

2. Cook the Lasagna Noodles

  1. If using regular lasagna noodles, cook them according to the package directions until al dente. Drain and lay flat on a clean surface or parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  2. If using no-boil noodles, skip this step.

3. Mix the Cheese Filling

  • In a medium bowl, combine ricotta, Parmesan, egg (if using), salt, pepper, and chopped basil or parsley. Stir until smooth and well mixed.

4. Assemble the Lasagna

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C) if not already on.
  2. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom.
  3. Layer 3 lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  4. Spread ⅓ of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
  5. Add ⅓ of the roasted vegetables on top.
  6. Spoon some marinara sauce over the veggies.
  7. Sprinkle with ⅓ of the mozzarella.
  8. Repeat the layers two more times: noodles, ricotta, veggies, sauce, mozzarella.
  9. Finish with a final layer of noodles, a bit of marinara, and the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan (if any).

5. Bake

  1. Cover the dish with foil (tent it slightly to avoid cheese sticking) and bake for 25 minutes.
  2. Remove the foil and bake uncovered for another 15–20 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.

6. Rest and Serve

  1. Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing. This helps it set and makes serving easier.
  2. Garnish with extra fresh herbs if desired.
